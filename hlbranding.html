HashLearn branding & design

We decided to revamp and give a fresh look to the HashLearn brand so that we could directly connect to and inspire our current users.

Overview

We decided to revamp and give a fresh look to the HashLearn brand so that we could directly connect to and inspire our current users.
We started with some research & looked at how other educational brands have solved their problems.

Research

About HashLearn

HashLearn is an on-demand tutoring app. It connects students with top tutors from IIT’s, NIT’s, BIT’s - instantly, anytime, anywhere. Apart from that if a student is a Passport member(the equivalent of Amazon Prime), they will get unlimited access to everything - Practice, Tests, Videos and Doubt Clearing.

Problems understanding

We tried to find out user’s needs and the problems they were facing with existing branding. We worked with our users on a daily basis and asked a couple of questions -

  • What problem do they face with the existing brand?
  • Do they want changes or not?
  • Which colour do they like or dislike? Why?
  • And some general questions to understand their day-to-day life

Concept/Initial sketches

After finishing the research part, I started with the initial sketching and created more than 50 variants.

We were happy with a couple of sketches. We sought feedback from users - based on which I made some changes and decided to go with this.

Process & Color Selection

We took inspiration from other brands and decided to choose the colour palate from the real world.
Since HashLearn is an educational company, the colour palette must also be inspirational and educational.
Our colour palette was generated entirely from real-life objects.
HashLearn yellow evokes happiness, youth and optimism, but is also attention-grabbing and affordable.
HashLearn blue gives off a feeling of maturity & trustworthiness.
